Problem 2:

Do men tend to have higher blood pressure than women? What about cholesterol?

From the questions, we create some hypothesizes to test:

H0a: men don't have higher blood pressure than women

H1a: men have higher blood pressure than women

H0b: men don't have higher cholesterol than women

H1b: men have higher blood cholesterol than women

In [46]:
male = data_frame[data_frame['sex']== 1]
female = data_frame[data_frame['sex']== 0]

We run two t-test for two hypothesizes with results:

In [47]:
result_a = ttest_ind(male['blood pressure'], female['blood pressure'])
result_b = ttest_ind(male['cholesterol'], female['cholesterol'])
In [48]:
plt.ylabel('Blood pressure')
plt.plot(male['blood pressure'], 'b^')
plt.plot(female['blood pressure'], 'ro')
plt.show()

P value from the test A:

In [49]:
print('pvalue = {}'.format(result_a.pvalue))

pvalue = 0.3246834797221041

With probability of H0a is pvalue > 0.005, so we cannot reject it

In [50]:

plt.plot(male['cholesterol'], 'b^')

plt.show()

P value from the test B:

In [51]:

pvalue = 0.000529966610622664

With probability of H0b is pvalue < 0.005, so we can reject it.

From the results based on this dataset, we can conclude that men have higher blood pressure than women but not sure about cholesterol.
